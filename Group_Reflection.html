Group Reflection

Our group name, Everywhere Australia, is derived from one of our biggest challenges – figuring out what time our meetings were in each of our local time zones! Being a team with individuals from literally ‘everywhere in Australia’, we were just about able to commit to meeting up over MS Teams twice a week to discuss the assignment, distribute work and understand how we were each progressing with our assigned tasks.

Although a challenge, as a team this is one aspect that we felt worked well towards working together to get the assignment completed. Within our weekly catchups we rotated the chair of the meeting between Konrad & Meghan, our structured agenda included: check-in on progress; what help is needed and what do we want done by the next meeting, with any additional issues being raised by team members in the catch-up. We feel as a group we were able to use these meetings effectively and equally distribute the work between ourselves.

Throughout the assignment we also believe that a strength of ours has been clear communication. For example, in our first meeting when we initially met as a team, both Vincent and Jason were open to say they would prefer not to chair the group but were happy to contribute to the weekly meetings and get on with the work. This clear communication within the group has let us progress with defined roles / personalities within the group.

With this in mind, one thing that has surprised us as a group is how easily we have got on and been able to work together without clashes. On reflection, the clear communication in the group has aided this as people have been open and honest and delivered work agreed to and if someone has a questions, is struggling or does not have the capacity to complete assigned work this has been communicated so other team members can assist in a timely manner. Opposed to say, a team member dropping off the face of the earth and the remaining members are not sure if they are continuing with their work or not.

One aspect we would look to improve for our next assignment is we would aim to have work completed at an earlier date, so we have more time to evaluate and fine tune our work before submitting the assignment. This is an aspect we will learn from and improve on our next assignment.

Meghan

I feel as a group we predominantly have worked very well to get this assignment over the line to a high standard. On reflection, comparing both assignment two and three, I feel our assignment three has been completed collectively to a higher standard. I am proud that we have worked better, setting shorter more regular deadlines for smaller amounts of work to complete the whole assignment to a good standard and on time. We have had some issues with communication in the group, if I were to do another group project, I would overcome this by reaching out to group members earlier in the project to get the momentum going with more time ahead, to deal with these issues sooner rather than later to not cause detriment to the team's performance. However, overall, I am happy with the contribution of the team and feel we have worked well throughout the duration of the Introduction to IT course.
